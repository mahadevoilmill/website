# Image Guide for Peanut Oil Website

This guide outlines the recommended images for each section of your professional Mahadev Oil Mill website.

## Image Locations & Recommendations

All images should be placed in the `/public/assets/` directory.

### 1. **peanut-hero.jpg** (Hero Section Background)
- **Location Used**: Hero section (full-screen background)
- **Recommended Style**: 
  - Peanut oil bottle or peanuts in a natural setting
  - Warm, inviting lighting with earthy tones
  - Size: 1920x1080px or larger (landscape)
  - Should convey premium quality and tradition
- **Example**: Beautiful bottle of golden peanut oil with peanuts scattered around, or peanut plants in a field with golden lighting

### 2. **peanut-farm.jpg** (Stats/Trust Section Background)
- **Location Used**: "Trusted by Thousands" section
- **Recommended Style**:
  - Aerial or landscape view of peanut farming fields
  - Golden/warm color palette with natural daylight
  - Size: 1920x1080px or larger
  - Should evoke trust, authenticity, and agricultural roots
- **Example**: Sprawling peanut fields with a warm sunset, farmers working in fields

### 3. **peanut-oil-bottle.jpg** (Products Section Background)
- **Location Used**: Product showcase section background
- **Recommended Style**:
  - Close-up or lifestyle shot of peanut oil bottles
  - Clean, professional product photography
  - Complementary items: measuring cups, peanuts, cooking ingredients
  - Size: 1920x1080px or larger
  - Light or neutral background to showcase products
- **Example**: Premium oil bottles with minimal decoration, fresh peanuts, or cooking scene

### 4. **peanut-process.jpg** (Process Section Background)
- **Location Used**: "From Field to Kitchen" section (4-step process)
- **Recommended Style**:
  - Collage or composite showing: sourcing → quality check → pressing → bottling
  - Or a traditional wood press in use
  - Warm, educational aesthetic
  - Size: 1920x1080px or larger
  - Dark enough to support white text overlay
- **Example**: Traditional wood press machine, peanuts being pressed, or step-by-step process images

### 5. **peanut-kitchen.jpg** (Comparison Section Background)
- **Location Used**: "Refined Oil vs Wood Pressed Oil" comparison table
- **Recommended Style**:
  - Warm kitchen scene showing oil being used in cooking
  - Family/home setting that conveys health and authenticity
  - Well-lit, inviting atmosphere
  - Size: 1920x1080px or larger
  - Dark enough for white text and table readability
- **Example**: Kitchen cooking scene with golden peanut oil being used, fresh vegetables, traditional cooking methods

### 6. **logo.jpeg** (Branding)
- **Location Used**: Header and footer
- **Current Size**: Displays at 50px height
- **Recommended Style**:
  - Square format logo of Mahadev Oil Mill brand
  - Should fit the brown/gold/natural color scheme
  - High quality for crisp display
  - Ideally PNG with transparency for better flexibility

## Color Palette
The website now uses a warm, earthy color scheme for peanut oil products:
- **Primary Brown**: `#8b4513` (warm earth tone)
- **Gold/Accent**: `#d4a574` (warm golden)
- **Secondary Brown**: `#5d3a1a` (darker alternative)
- **Cream/Light Background**: `#fef5e7` (warm off-white)

## Image Sourcing Recommendations
1. **Stock Photo Sites**: Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay
   - Search: "peanut oil", "farming", "kitchen cooking", "agricultural fields"
2. **High Quality**: Look for professional, well-lit images
3. **Licensing**: Ensure free-to-use or appropriate licensing
4. **Format**: Use JPG for photos, PNG for graphics with transparency
5. **Optimization**: Compress images for web (use tools like TinyPNG or ImageOptim)

## Best Practices
- All images should be minimum 1920px width for desktop quality
- Provide mobile-optimized versions if using responsive images
- Ensure images don't make the site feel cluttered
- Maintain consistent color grading across all images for cohesive look
- Add alt text descriptions for accessibility

## Image Naming Convention
Keep the exact filenames as shown in the guide - the website code references them specifically:
